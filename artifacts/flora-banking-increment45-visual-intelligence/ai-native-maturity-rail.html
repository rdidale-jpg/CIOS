UK Banking → AI-native bank

What does the AI-native bank look like?

An AI-native bank uses trusted data, governed AI and redesigned work to anticipate needs, automate routine activity safely and give humans full context for judgement.

AI-native maturity rail

Five stage visual progression from legacy constrained to AI-native bank. Only the AI-assisted stage is expanded by default; select a stage to inspect details.

1 Legacy constrained

Old systems constrain change.

  • Customer: Customers repeat information and wait for hand-offs.
  • Employee: Employees reconcile manual workarounds.
  • Operating model: Function-led work with after-the-fact controls.
  • Constraint: Legacy simplification and evidence debt.
2 Digitally enabled

Digital journeys exist but remain fragmented.

  • Customer: Customers self-serve common tasks but exceptions are clumsy.
  • Employee: Employees still swivel-chair across queues.
  • Operating model: Digital teams improve channels more than the enterprise.
  • Constraint: Data ownership and service integration.
3 Integrated and data-led

Reusable data and workflows connect channels.

  • Customer: Customers get more consistent contextual service.
  • Employee: Employees see better case and customer context.
  • Operating model: Journey and data-product ownership emerges.
  • Constraint: Scaling governed AI safely.
4 AI-assisted enterprise

AI supports staff and controls with human accountability.

  • Customer: Customers get faster resolution and better next actions.
  • Employee: Employees use copilots and focus on exceptions.
  • Operating model: Controls, data and delivery are redesigned around AI-assisted work.
  • Constraint: Trust, resilience and operating-model redesign.
5 AI-native bank

AI is embedded in routine processes with accountable humans.

  • Customer: Needs are anticipated and routine issues resolve automatically.
  • Employee: Humans focus on judgement, empathy and relationships.
  • Operating model: Adaptive, evidence-led operating model with continuous control.
  • Constraint: Sustaining fairness, resilience and ecosystem accountability.
