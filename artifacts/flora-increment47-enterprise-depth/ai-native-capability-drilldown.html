Customer experience

Current traditional state: fragmented manual work and channel/process separation.

Digitally enabled state: customer and staff journeys are partly digital but exceptions remain manual.

AI-assisted state: copilots, analytics and controls support employees with human accountability.

AI-native state: adaptive processes use real-time data with continuous controls and human exception handling.

Customer impact: faster, more contextual and inclusive service.

Employee impact: fewer hand-offs and more judgement work.

Operating-model change: workflow, data, controls and suppliers managed as one system.

Cost impact: lower avoidable manual demand; investment shifts to data, resilience and ecosystem control.

Major barriers: data quality, legacy architecture, explainability, resilience, inclusion and accountable ownership.

Likely opportunities: Customer Experience Transformation and Outsourcing; Deposits and Customer Value Management

Relevant suppliers: AWS, Accenture, Google Cloud, HSBC Group technology ecosystem
