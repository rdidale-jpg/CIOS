## Purpose

Founder Knowledge represents strategic reasoning, future-state design, commercial hypotheses and enterprise reinvention thinking developed through structured design conversations.

It is distinct from public evidence, Enterprise Intelligence, architecture and operational commissions. Founder Knowledge records the originating design material; it does not establish public evidence, accepted architecture or an operational instruction.

This document governs preservation and recovery only. It does not rewrite the methodology, modernise terminology, merge Founder Knowledge with CIOS architecture, promote Founder reasoning into accepted architecture, or treat Founder reasoning as public evidence.

## Knowledge Types

Founder Knowledge is classified into the following four categories. Classification describes the asset's role; it does not accept, validate or reinterpret its content.

| Knowledge type | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Design Method | A structured approach for reasoning about enterprise change, growth or reinvention. | Enterprise Growth Method |
| Blueprint | A future-state design that applies a method to an enterprise or industry context. | Banking Reinvention Blueprint |
| Design Evidence | Original material that records the development of Founder Knowledge. | Original GPT conversations, workshop notes, iterations and alternative models |
| Executive Artefacts | Materials prepared to communicate Founder Knowledge to executive audiences. | Presentation decks, white papers and executive summaries |

## Recovery Workflow

Every recovered conversation passes through the following process. The workflow permits classification and preservation only; no interpretation beyond classification is performed.

```text
Conversation
    ↓
Identify Topic
    ↓
Identify Knowledge Type
    ↓
Extract Stable Principles
    ↓
Extract Assumptions
    ↓
Extract Alternatives
    ↓
Extract Unknowns
    ↓
Register Asset
    ↓
Link Evidence
    ↓
Await Recovery
```

### Recovery Record Controls

- Retain the source conversation and identify its topic before assigning a knowledge type.
- Record stable principles, assumptions, alternatives and unknowns as extracted material, without resolving, updating or endorsing them.
- Register an asset only when a source evidences it; do not create inferred assets.
- Link each register entry to its available design evidence and leave incomplete material in recovery.

## Recovery Status

Every asset uses the following simple maturity model:

```text
Discovered
    ↓
Recovering
    ↓
Recovered
    ↓
Governed
    ↓
Integrated
```

This lifecycle is about preservation, not acceptance. A status records the state of recovery and handling; it is not an evidence rating, architecture decision, production approval or endorsement of the asset's content.

## Design Provenance

Every recovered asset must record:

- source conversation(s);
- approximate creation period, if known;
- related presentation material;
- related blueprint(s);
- related Enterprise Growth Method components;
- recovery confidence; and
- outstanding gaps.

Where a field is not known, record it as unknown rather than supplying an interpretation. Provenance records preserve the relationship between an asset and its design evidence.

## Flora Relationship

Founder Knowledge is not loaded into the public-domain Researcher. Instead, it becomes an input to future Reinvention Blueprint construction.

This relationship does not make Founder Knowledge public evidence or an accepted architectural component. Any future use remains subject to the applicable blueprint and governance process.

## Unknowns

Maintain an explicit record of:

- missing conversations;
- incomplete blueprints;
- unresolved design questions; and
- assets referenced but not yet recovered.

Unknowns are preservation records. They must not be filled by inference, terminology updates or retrospective methodological interpretation.
