# Flora Product Maturity Review — Executive Intelligence Experience Sprint

**Status:** current sprint review  
**Date:** 2026-07-02

## What improved in this sprint

- Flora now leads major pages with judgement: why the situation matters, why now, what to do next, confidence / conviction and then supporting evidence.
- Organisation reports were reframed around an Executive Intelligence Brief, Flora's View, recommended next conversation, commercial priority card and an inspectable reasoning chain.
- Score arithmetic, evidence counts, trace IDs and raw scrape material were moved into analyst diagnostics or drill-down sections.
- Evidence presentation now uses Evidence Library / Evidence Cards language and separates executive-grade evidence from context, noisy diagnostics and rejected evidence.

## What still feels like engineering output

- Some lower-page diagnostics still expose implementation vocabulary such as score components, source status and runtime collection metadata.
- The deterministic templates can still sound rigid when evidence is sparse.
- Portfolio and Observatory groupings are rule-based and would benefit from longitudinal movement history when available.

## What now feels like executive intelligence

- The top of each organisation page answers the executive question before showing mechanics.
- Recommendations are tied to a primary hypothesis and explicitly state what not to overclaim.
- Weak or seeded fallback evidence is treated as a learning action rather than a confident recommendation.
- Competing hypotheses make uncertainty visible and convert gaps into validation questions.

## Remaining gaps to reach Executive Intelligence Officer maturity

- Stronger longitudinal memory is needed to distinguish genuine momentum from repeated static evidence.
- Contradictory evidence should become more actively collected rather than only exposed when already present.
- Executive role targeting should mature by sector and account-specific governance structures.
- Evidence quality gating should continue to reject page furniture, menus and boilerplate before executive rendering.

## Recommended next strategic sprint

**Sprint theme:** Evidence-led learning loop and contradiction hunting.

Recommended scope:

1. Add deterministic contradiction search plans for each active hypothesis.
2. Track hypothesis state changes over time with visible strengthening / weakening reasons.
3. Add account-specific executive stakeholder maps from governed evidence only.
4. Expand product guardrails so executive sections never show raw scrape, repeated thesis text or seeded fallback as strong live evidence.
5. Update this document after every future runtime sprint.
