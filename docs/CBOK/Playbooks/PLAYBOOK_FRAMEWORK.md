## Executive Summary

The Commercial Intelligence Playbook Library governs how CIOS turns observed signals into context-specific commercial recommendations. It provides reusable sector, capability, executive, competitor, pattern, anti-pattern and proposition assets that Flora may consume in future versions. The library is not application code and does not define runtime behaviour.

## Purpose

The library shall help commercial teams identify when a signal indicates pressure, when pressure may create demand, which stakeholders matter, which AI reinvention propositions fit, and what action should follow.

## Scope

In scope: governed knowledge assets, recommendation heuristics, confidence guidance, evidence expectations and traceability. Out of scope: SDK packages, Python code, scoring algorithms, automated decisions and unsupported external claims.

## Governing Principle

Flora should not recommend generic actions. Flora should recommend context-specific actions derived from signals, commercial pressure, AI reinvention potential, competitive context, stakeholder influence and the user’s Commercial DNA.

## Playbook Types

| Type | Definition | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| Sector playbook | Sector-specific pressure, stakeholder, trigger and proposition guidance. | Interpret market context. |
| Capability playbook | Capability-specific pain, data and outcome guidance. | Shape value conversations. |
| Executive playbook | Role-specific priorities, concerns and influence guidance. | Tailor stakeholder engagement. |
| Competitor playbook | Validatable hypotheses about rival positioning. | Prepare differentiation. |
| Proposition playbook | Reusable offer guidance. | Select next best commercial motion. |
| Pattern | Repeated signal-to-demand heuristic. | Increase pursuit confidence. |
| Anti-pattern | Repeated warning heuristic. | Avoid weak pursuits or reshape access. |

## Signal-to-Recommendation Method

1. Identify signals and evidence quality.
2. Map signals to patterns or anti-patterns.
3. Assess commercial pressure and AIRI fit.
4. Select relevant sector, capability, executive and competitor playbooks.
5. Filter by Commercial DNA and differentiation.
6. Select propositions and next actions.
7. State confidence, assumptions, validation needs and review path.

## Flora Usage

Flora should cite the playbook identifiers it used, avoid overclaiming, present hypotheses as hypotheses, and separate recommended actions from evidence gaps. Flora must not infer private intent from public signals alone.

## Confidence and Evidence Expectations

Recommendations should carry scientific confidence for the underlying knowledge and operational confidence for its readiness in sales use. Public reports, regulated disclosures, customer statements, procurement notices and validated CRM evidence may raise confidence. Social noise or single weak signals should normally remain low confidence.

## Review and Update Process

Playbooks shall be reviewed when major sector regulation changes, repeated recommendation outcomes contradict guidance, new propositions are approved, competitor positioning changes materially, or CBOK standards are updated.

## Traceability Expectations

Each recommendation should preserve the chain: signal, evidence, pattern or anti-pattern, playbook, proposition, recommendation, user action, outcome evidence and CBOK refinement.

## Governance Requirements

Playbooks shall use professional language, avoid invented citations, avoid defamatory claims, distinguish hypotheses from standards, and maintain version and review history. Competitor statements must be cautious and validated before customer-facing use.
