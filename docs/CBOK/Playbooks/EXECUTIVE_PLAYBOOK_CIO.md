## Role Definition

technology strategy, cyber, platforms, integration and delivery risk

## Typical Priorities

Value, risk, resilience, accountable delivery, measurable outcomes and stakeholder confidence.

## Typical Pressures

Public commitments, cost pressure, legacy constraints, cyber and data risk, regulatory scrutiny and executive performance expectations.

## AI Reinvention Concerns

Responsible AI, business ownership, data readiness, adoption, controls, integration, reputational risk and measurable ROI.

## Buying Influence

This role may sponsor, shape, block or validate AI reinvention depending on authority and pain ownership.

## Signals That the Stakeholder Is Important

Public statements, committee role, transformation ownership, new appointment, budget accountability, incident accountability or named programme sponsorship.

## Evidence Sources

Annual reports, regulated disclosures, investor materials, procurement notices, leadership announcements, public speeches and validated relationship notes.

## Conversation Themes

Connect AI reinvention to the role’s measurable priorities, evidence gaps and decision constraints.

## Challenger-Style Constructive Tension Themes

Respectfully challenge whether current operating models can meet public commitments without data, automation and governance improvements.

## Recommended Assets

Sector playbook, relevant capability playbook, AI Reinvention Assessment, AI Governance and Risk Framework, data foundation briefing and proposition one-pager.

## Common Objections

Too much change, insufficient data, uncertain ROI, risk exposure, incumbent roadmap and lack of capacity.

## Common Mistakes

Do not lead with technology jargon, ignore the role’s KPI, overstate AI certainty or bypass legitimate decision routes.

## NED Network Considerations Where Relevant

Use only legitimate public information and treat board links as influence context, not evidence of private intent.
