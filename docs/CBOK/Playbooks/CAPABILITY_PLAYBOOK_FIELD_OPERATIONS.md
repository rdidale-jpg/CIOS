## Capability Definition

## Business Outcomes

Improved capacity, schedule adherence, safety, first-time fix and productivity, with measurable value and accountable ownership.

## Typical Pain Points

capacity, schedule adherence, safety, first-time fix and productivity

## AI Reinvention Opportunities

Agentic workflow, predictive insight, decision support, knowledge assistants, anomaly detection, automation and governed human-in-the-loop recommendations.

## Common Signals

Backlogs, service failures, cost-out targets, legacy platforms, audit findings, data investment, executive refresh and customer or workforce pressure.

## Typical Data Requirements

Process events, case/work/order history, master data, knowledge content, performance metrics, controls and outcome labels with ownership and quality checks.

COO, CIO, CDO, CFO and capability-specific directors.
