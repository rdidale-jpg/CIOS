## Sector Definition

Utilities includes regulated water, power distribution and essential service providers.

## Sector Scope

This playbook covers commercial recommendations where signals indicate pressure around regulation, customer trust, environmental performance, leakage, resilience, asset management, field operations, affordability and capital investment cycles.

## Typical Commercial Pressures

regulation, customer trust, environmental performance, leakage, resilience, asset management, field operations, affordability and capital investment cycles

## Typical External Triggers

Regulatory determinations, market disruption, investor commitments, service incidents, technology shifts, competitor announcements and leadership changes.

## Typical Executive Priorities

Resilience, cost transformation, customer outcomes, regulatory confidence, data-enabled operations, cyber risk, sustainability and AI value.

## AI Reinvention Themes

Predictive operations, intelligent workflow, agentic service, decision support, data foundations, governance, personalisation and AI-enabled productivity.

## High-Value Capability Areas

Customer operations, field operations, contact centre, asset management, finance, data platform, cyber resilience and executive reporting.

## Common Buying Triggers

Public commitments, mandated performance improvement, legacy platform events, executive refresh, customer trust decline and capital programme mobilisation.

## Stakeholder Map

CIO owns technology risk; COO owns operational outcomes; CDO owns data and digital value; CFO owns investment discipline; NEDs influence risk appetite and governance.

## Board and NED Considerations

Boards may focus on service resilience, public accountability, safety, cyber, AI governance, affordability, reputation and evidence of delivery.

## Regulatory / Market Considerations

Recommendations should reflect regulatory obligations, market economics and customer-impact evidence without asserting legal advice.

## Likely Competitor Positioning

Large integrators may position around transformation scale, cloud/data partnerships, managed services, sector credentials and AI accelerators; validate in-account evidence.

## Differentiation Opportunities

Differentiate through sector-specific outcomes, explainable AI governance, referenceable delivery, operational empathy, data readiness and measurable value cases.

## Early Warning Signals

Performance notices, complaint movements, churn, outage patterns, capex changes, board appointments, procurement notices and public AI/data commitments.

## Recommended Executive Conversations

Ask what public commitments are hardest to deliver, where operational data blocks action, and which AI use cases would change customer or asset outcomes.

## Recommended Workshops

Run an AI reinvention opportunity workshop, pressure-to-proposition mapping session, data readiness review and executive value case sprint.

## Recommended Thought Leadership

Use short, sector-specific briefings on governed AI reinvention, operational resilience, customer trust and data foundations.

## Common Mistakes

Do not recommend generic AI pilots, ignore regulatory context, overlook change capacity, or treat public pressure as budget evidence.
