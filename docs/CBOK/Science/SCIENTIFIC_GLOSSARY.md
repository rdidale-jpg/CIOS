## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Evidence | Reviewed information that supports, challenges or qualifies a CBOK knowledge artefact. |
| Claim | A declarative statement asserting that something may be true. |
| Observation | A recorded fact, event, measurement or practitioner-noted occurrence in a stated context. |
| Hypothesis | A falsifiable proposition intended for structured validation. |
| Law | A durable relationship repeatedly observed across commercial contexts and approved within defined limits. |
| Model | A structured representation of entities, relationships, behaviours, decisions or mechanisms. |
| Framework | An organising structure for concepts, processes, artefacts or evaluation methods. |
| Standard | A controlled normative document that establishes required or recommended practice. |
| Reference Model | A model approved as a common reference for analysis, alignment or implementation. |
| Scientific Confidence | The assessed strength of scientific support for a claim, model, law or artefact. |
| Operational Confidence | The assessed readiness of an artefact for governed operational, engineering or product use. |
| Validation | The structured assessment of whether evidence supports a claim within stated limits. |
| Falsifiability | The property of being testable in a way that could show a claim to be false or inadequate. |
| Traceability | The maintained chain from evidence through knowledge artefacts to engineering, applications and operational evidence. |
| Conformance | Demonstrated alignment with required provisions of a governing standard. |
