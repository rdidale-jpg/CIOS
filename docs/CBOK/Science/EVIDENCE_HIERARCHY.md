## Purpose

This document defines evidence levels used to evaluate CBOK scientific knowledge. Evidence levels describe strength, not certainty. Reviewers shall also consider relevance, quality, recency, bias, scope and contradictory evidence.

## Evidence Levels

| Level | Definition | Strength | Limitations | Example | When It May Be Used | When It Is Insufficient |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| E0 — Unverified assertion | A statement with no reviewed support. | Useful only as a prompt for inquiry. | No provenance, validation or reliability. | A stakeholder says a workflow is confusing without examples. | Intake notes, idea capture, research backlog. | Any approved claim, standard or engineering baseline. |
| E1 — Expert opinion | Judgement from a qualified person or group. | May identify plausible mechanisms and risks quickly. | Subject to bias, incomplete context and authority overreach. | A domain reviewer predicts that clearer explanations will improve adoption. | Early scoping, risk assessment, hypothesis formation. | Sole support for high-impact standards or production behaviour. |
| E2 — Practitioner observation | Recorded observation from practice in a specific context. | Grounded in operational reality. | May be anecdotal, local or confounded. | A customer success team records repeated onboarding objections. | Claims, working papers, exploratory pilots. | Broad laws, general models or unrestricted recommendations. |
| E3 — Single validated case study | One structured case with reviewed method and findings. | Provides contextual validation and practical detail. | Limited generalisability and replication. | A controlled pilot shows an onboarding intervention reduced drop-off in one team. | Candidate artefacts and bounded recommendations. | Claims of broad applicability without additional evidence. |
| E4 — Multiple case studies | Several reviewed cases across contexts or periods. | Shows recurrence and begins to test boundaries. | Cases may still share hidden selection bias. | Three business units report similar adoption improvements under comparable controls. | Patterns, models and controlled-use guidance. | Established laws or standards with large downstream risk unless supported by review. |
| E5 — Multiple validated organisations | Evidence from multiple organisations or materially distinct operating contexts. | Strong support for external validity within stated limits. | Still may not cover all market, cultural or system conditions. | Independent organisations validate the same commercial decision pattern. | Laws, reference models, standards and engineering baselines. | Universal claims or irreversible decisions without contradiction review. |
| E6 — Independent meta-analysis or widely established evidence base | Independent synthesis or mature body of convergent evidence. | Highest CBOK evidence strength for durable knowledge. | Quality depends on included studies and applicability to CBOK context. | A reviewed evidence base consistently supports a behavioural mechanism used in a model. | Foundational standards, high-impact engineering constraints. | Claims outside the analysed population, mechanism or context. |

## Use Rules

- Evidence level shall be recorded separately from scientific and operational confidence.
- Higher evidence levels may still be inappropriate if the evidence does not match the claim scope.
- Lower evidence levels may support exploratory work but must be labelled accordingly.
