## Purpose

This checklist supports consistent review of CBOK scientific artefacts before promotion, approval or engineering consumption.

## Checklist

| Review Area | Required Check |
|---|---|
| Terminology consistency | Terms match the Scientific Glossary, CBOK definitions and related standards. |
| Evidence quality | Evidence level, provenance, relevance, method, limitations and scope are documented. |
| Confidence rating | Scientific and operational confidence are assigned separately with rationale. |
| Falsifiability | Hypotheses and causal claims identify what evidence would challenge or disprove them. |
| Contradictory evidence | Known counterevidence, failed cases and uncertainty are disclosed. |
| Ethical considerations | Bias, fairness, user impact, safety, privacy and misuse risks are considered where relevant. |
| Engineering impact | Downstream architecture, SDK, application, workflow and operational effects are identified. |
| Traceability | Upstream evidence and downstream consumers are linked with stable identifiers. |
| Duplication check | Existing CBOK artefacts have been checked to avoid conflicting or redundant records. |
| Review owner | Accountable owner and reviewer roles are recorded. |
| Next review date | Next review date or trigger is documented. |

## Review Decision

Reviewers shall record one decision: approve, approve with constraints, request amendment, request further evidence, reject or archive. Conditional approvals shall identify constraints, owner and due date.
