## Purpose

This document defines promotion rules for CBOK scientific knowledge. Promotion increases authority only when evidence, review and traceability requirements are met.

## Promotion Path

```text
Observation
→ Claim
→ Working Paper
→ Literature Review
→ Hypothesis
→ Experiment
→ Validation Report
→ Commercial Law / Model / Reference Model
→ Standard
→ Engineering Standard
→ SDK / Platform / Application
```

## Promotion Rules

### Observations Become Claims

An observation may become a claim when the author states a clear assertion, identifies context and links the assertion to one or more observations. The claim shall preserve source limitations and must not imply general validity beyond the observed context.

### Claims Become Working Papers

A claim may become a working paper when it has an owner, scope, initial evidence, related terms, known assumptions and a reason for further investigation. Duplicate or overlapping claims should be consolidated before promotion.

### Working Papers Become Hypotheses

A working paper may produce a hypothesis when the proposed relationship is falsifiable, bounded and capable of validation. The hypothesis shall identify expected outcomes, relevant variables, evaluation method and potential contradictory findings.

### Hypotheses Become Experiments

A hypothesis may become an experiment when reviewers approve a protocol, measures, ethical constraints, success criteria and analysis plan. Experiments should be proportionate to downstream risk and shall record limitations.

### Validation Reports Support Laws or Models

A validation report may support a law, model or reference model when evidence demonstrates the claimed relationship, mechanism or structure within stated boundaries. Reviewers shall assess evidence quality, confidence, counterevidence and applicability.

### Laws and Models Become Standards

A law, model or reference model may become a standard when it is approved for normative use, has sufficient scientific and operational confidence, includes traceability and has clear engineering or governance implications.

### Engineering Consumes Approved Standards

Engineering may consume approved standards when the standard states implementation constraints, downstream mapping, operational confidence, owner and review cadence. Engineering must preserve traceability to the CBOK source and return operational evidence to the governance process.

## Exceptions

Governance may approve constrained exceptions for urgent operational learning or low-risk pilots. Exceptions shall be documented, time-bounded and reviewed before broader adoption.
