## Purpose

This document defines the lifecycle through which CBOK knowledge matures. Each stage shall have an owner, status, evidence basis and exit decision before promotion.

## Lifecycle Stages

| Stage | Meaning | Entry Criteria | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| Idea | A possible area of knowledge or inquiry. | Need, question or signal identified. | Recorded as observation, deferred or rejected. |
| Observation | A contextual record of something seen, measured or reported. | Source, context and date or period captured. | Converted to claim, linked to existing artefact or archived. |
| Claim | A reviewable assertion that may be true. | Statement is clear, bounded and linked to rationale or observation. | Accepted into working paper, revised, rejected or archived. |
| Working Paper | A structured exploration of claims, assumptions and implications. | Owner, scope, claims and initial evidence identified. | Promoted to literature review, hypothesis or candidate artefact; or returned for revision. |
| Literature Review | A review of relevant internal and external knowledge without inventing unsupported citations. | Research question, search scope and included evidence recorded. | Supports hypothesis, model, law candidate or rejection. |
| Hypothesis | A falsifiable proposition with expected relationship and scope. | Variables, expected outcome, limits and evidence rationale stated. | Experiment approved, revised or rejected. |
| Experiment | A structured validation activity. | Protocol, measures, ethics and success criteria approved. | Results recorded in validation report. |
| Validation Report | Reviewed findings from experiments, case studies or evidence synthesis. | Evidence collected, analysed and limitations documented. | Supports candidate artefact, further work or rejection. |
| Candidate Artefact | A proposed model, law, reference model, framework or standard ready for formal review. | Evidence summary, confidence rating, traceability and downstream impact documented. | Approved, returned for revision or rejected. |
| Approved Artefact | A governed artefact accepted for defined use. | Review decision recorded with constraints and owner. | Promoted to standard, maintained, superseded or archived. |
| Standard | A normative baseline for authors, reviewers or engineering consumers. | Approval authority accepts evidence, traceability and operational mapping. | Maintained, superseded or archived. |
| Superseded | Replaced by a newer controlled artefact. | Replacement identified with rationale and migration guidance. | Archived when no longer referenced by active work. |
| Archived | Preserved for history but inactive. | Retention need confirmed and active use ended. | Reopened only by governance decision. |

## Lifecycle Controls

- Promotion shall require explicit review evidence, not only author preference.
- Rejection and archival decisions should preserve rationale.
- Superseded artefacts must retain links to replacements.
