# MOD-CDT-05 — Owner Acceptance Decision

**Enterprise:** United Kingdom Ministry of Defence and material wider Defence enterprise interfaces  
**Release accepted:** MOD Commercial Digital Twin v1.3  
**Decision date:** 10 July 2026  
**Owner:** Rob / CIOS  
**Decision:** Accept with constraints  
**Decision class:** Commercial strategy and governed research baseline  
**Related architecture decision:** ADR-009

## Decision

MOD-CDT v1.3 is accepted as the current governed research baseline for:

- MOD enterprise-priority decisions;
- retained broader flagship sequencing;
- executive validation and account-learning decisions;
- bounded discussion of pressures, mechanisms and candidate reinvention seams.

The intelligence state is accepted. The v1.3 publication and governance process is not adopted as the default production pattern for future Commercial Digital Twins.

## Accepted boundaries

The release is not accepted for:

- Provider Fit conclusions;
- provider-specific flagship selection;
- Pursue, Redirect, Reject or funded opportunity decisions;
- sponsor outreach;
- procurement or investment approval;
- wallet-share inference;
- claims requiring internal operational or classified evidence.

The existing Pending account decisions remain Pending.

## Accepted limitations

The following remain explicit rather than hidden incompleteness:

- Provider Fit is not assessed;
- sponsor and receiving-owner routes are not validated;
- outside-in operating mechanisms require internal validation;
- public commercial routes do not prove eligibility, access or workshare;
- no enterprise-wide Burning Platform is accepted;
- no provider-specific Pursue recommendation is accepted.

## Process learning

The release demonstrated:

- materially improved MOD-specific pain and pressure analysis;
- stronger causal and response-effectiveness reasoning;
- useful separation of enterprise need, accessibility and Provider Fit;
- excessive elapsed time and document-production overhead when full release governance is applied to routine research.

This learning supports ADR-009 Progressive Assurance.

## Required next action

Return to Flora runtime testing using MOD v1.3 as an accepted baseline.

The first test should ingest or reconstruct the accepted governed state and then perform a bounded incremental refresh without regenerating the complete release package.

## Review trigger

Review this acceptance when:

- a material MOD evidence change occurs;
- Provider Fit or account-route evidence is supplied;
- the Twin is promoted for external or provider-specific use;
- the accepted baseline is superseded by a later governed release.
