## 1. Purpose

This Contract defines the minimum research depth, evidence discipline, artefacts, reasoning and acceptance criteria required to construct a Commercial Digital Twin Blueprint.

It operationalises the CIOS Reference Architecture, EI-001 Enterprise Model Specification, EI-002 Enterprise Knowledge Graph, EI-003 Enterprise Behaviour Model, EI-012 Enterprise Observation Model, FP-009 Hypothesis Validation Standard and the CIOS Chief Architect Handbook. It does not replace those owning documents or redefine their canonical objects.

The Contract exists to prevent a Digital Twin from becoming a polished company profile, financial-report summary or generic transformation report.

A compliant Twin must explain:

1. who controls and influences the enterprise;
2. what the board must defend, fix, deliver and prove;
3. which strategic hopes and bets management is relying on;
4. how revenue, cost, cash and capital are actually produced;
5. how business units, people, processes, technology, data, suppliers and regulation interact;
6. where value is being created, destroyed, delayed or put at risk;
7. how those mechanisms are likely to shape the next financial release;
8. where a flagship AI-enabled transformation could materially alter the trajectory;
9. what evidence supports that view, what contradicts it and what remains Unknown.

The target user is an elite strategic commercial professional shaping material enterprise reinvention before procurement.

## 3. Core doctrine

The Contract applies these principles throughout:

> Evidence proves change.  
> Observations remember change.  
> Enterprise Models accumulate change.  
> Reports are views.

Additional rules:

- Reported fact, calculation, inference, Signal, Hypothesis, forecast and Recommendation MUST remain distinguishable.
- Human-supplied knowledge MUST be labelled by contributor and date.
- Strong Recommendations MUST have inspectable lineage.
- Unknowns and Contradictions MUST remain visible.
- A provider or research-agent output is candidate intelligence until governed acceptance.
- Depth MUST come from causal explanation, not document length.
- Where conviction is incomplete, the next action SHOULD maximise learning before selling.
- A transformation need MUST NOT be confused with a commercially accessible opportunity.

---

## 4. Definition of the Blueprint

A Commercial Digital Twin Blueprint is a time-bound, evidence-backed outside-in reconstruction of how an enterprise is controlled, funded, organised, operated, changed and likely to behave.

It is not yet the durable Enterprise Model. It is a candidate view capable of becoming part of a Living Commercial Digital Twin after evidence, Observations and accepted model updates are governed.

Every Blueprint MUST have:

- enterprise and modelling boundary;
- effective date and reporting period;
- source cut-off date;
- version;
- evidence register;
- coverage and confidence statement;
- explicit Unknowns and Contradictions;
- labelled human contributions;
- next refresh triggers.

---

## 5. The causal spine

Every material conclusion SHOULD be traceable through this causal spine:

```text
Ownership or external influence
→ Board agenda
→ Burning platform or strategic bet
→ Business-unit economics
→ Revenue, cost, cash or risk driver
→ Process, workforce, platform, data or supplier mechanism
→ Operating KPI or leading indicator
→ Expected financial or strategic consequence
→ Next-release implication
→ Transformation requirement
→ Specific role for AI
```

A conclusion that cannot be connected to at least one part of this spine MUST be marked as context, not transformation intelligence.

## 6. Required depth model

The Blueprint MUST assess the enterprise through Levels 0 to 6. The levels are cumulative rather than sequential reporting sections.

### Level 0 — Corporate control, ownership, influence and agendas

**Purpose:** establish who and what shapes enterprise decisions before interpreting management reporting.

The Blueprint MUST examine, where material:

- parent and subsidiary relationships;
- controlling and major shareholders;
- state ownership or government interest;
- activist investors;
- institutional investor concentration;
- lenders, bondholders and rating agencies;
- pension obligations;
- joint-venture partners;
- major customers;
- critical suppliers;
- regulators and licence authorities;
- political stakeholders;
- unions and employee representatives;
- board and committee structure;
- remuneration and incentive design;
- national-security or critical-infrastructure obligations;
- media, public and reputational pressure.

For every material influence, record:

- actor;
- formal or informal mechanism of influence;
- apparent agenda;
- decisions or outcomes influenced;
- time horizon;
- supporting Evidence;
- alternative interpretation;
- confidence;
- status as reported, inferred, human-supplied or Unknown.

The Blueprint MUST NOT present alleged hidden agendas as fact.

**Mandatory Level 0 artefacts:**

1. corporate ownership map;
2. governance and committee map;
3. influence network;
4. agenda and incentive map;
5. external constraint map;
6. unresolved ownership and influence questions.

### Level 1 — Board agenda, strategic tension and burning platforms

**Purpose:** explain what could materially change leadership confidence, capital allocation, guidance, enterprise value or licence to operate.

The board agenda MUST be reconstructed through four lenses:

#### Must defend

Examples include:

- licence to operate;
- critical customers;
- market position;
- cash generation;
- credit rating;
- dividend capacity;
- service resilience;
- strategic assets;
- regulatory standing;
- reputation.

#### Must fix

Examples include:

- deteriorating revenue engines;
- structurally high cost;
- poor service or operational performance;
- customer attrition;
- legacy technology;
- security or resilience exposure;
- failed transformation;
- debt pressure;
- productivity gaps;
- regulatory intervention;
- weak controls.

#### Must deliver

Examples include:

- public guidance;
- cost savings;
- restructuring;
- capex programmes;
- service improvements;
- platform modernisation;
- workforce change;
- integration benefits;
- market-share or growth ambitions.

#### Must prove

Examples include:

- strategy is working;
- investment is producing returns;
- transformation benefits are real;
- growth is sustainable;
- cost reduction is not damaging service;
- leadership can execute;
- AI or technology investments are moving beyond pilots.

A condition qualifies as a burning platform only where Evidence indicates:

1. material consequence;
2. time pressure or approaching deadline;
3. worsening trajectory or credibility exposure;
4. insufficient response from the current operating model;
5. board or executive accountability.

**Mandatory Level 1 artefacts:**

1. board agenda map;
2. board anxiety register;
3. burning platform register;
4. public promise and commitment register;
5. credibility-at-risk register;
6. deadline and consequence map;
7. board-level Unknowns and Contradictions.

### Level 2 — Strategic hopes, enterprise bets and economic engine

**Purpose:** establish what management is relying on to improve the enterprise and whether the underlying economics support that hope.

For every material strategic bet, record:

- proposition;
- intended outcome;
- executive sponsor;
- business units affected;
- investment required;
- expected benefits;
- time horizon;
- revenue, cost, cash and capital dependencies;
- process, workforce, platform and data dependencies;
- supplier or regulatory dependencies;
- evidence of progress;
- leading indicators;
- contradictory evidence;
- invalidation conditions;
- confidence.

#### Revenue architecture

For every material revenue stream, the Blueprint SHOULD reconstruct:

- customer base;
- customer additions and losses;
- churn and retention;
- price;
- discounting;
- volume or usage;
- product and customer mix;
- channel;
- geography;
- contract duration;
- recurring versus transactional revenue;
- order intake or backlog where relevant;
- market share;
- regulation;
- competitor effects;
- foreign exchange;
- acquisition and disposal effects.

The Blueprint SHOULD explain the underlying drivers of the substantial majority of enterprise revenue. Any unexplained material portion MUST be a coverage gap.

#### Cost architecture

Costs MUST be analysed by:

- business unit and function;
- workforce and contractors;
- technology and data;
- infrastructure or network;
- customer acquisition and service;
- property;
- energy and commodities;
- suppliers and outsourcing;
- regulatory and compliance activity;
- depreciation and amortisation;
- restructuring;
- financing;
- central overhead.

Costs SHOULD also be classified by:

- fixed, variable or semi-variable;
- direct, shared or allocated;
- near-term controllable or structurally committed;
- run, change, growth, compliance or remediation;
- operating expense, capital expenditure or exceptional item.

#### Cash and capital

The Blueprint MUST explain:

- operating cash conversion;
- working capital;
- maintenance versus growth capex;
- technology and transformation capitalisation;
- leases;
- pensions;
- restructuring cash;
- interest and tax;
- debt and refinancing;
- dividends and buybacks;
- acquisitions and disposals;
- liquidity;
- discretionary investment capacity.

**Mandatory Level 2 artefacts:**

1. strategic bet register;
2. revenue driver trees;
3. cost driver trees;
4. profit-to-cash bridge;
5. capital allocation map;
6. value-pool map;
7. bet-to-outcome dependency map;
8. confidence and contradiction assessment.

### Level 3 — Organisational anatomy and operating reality

**Purpose:** drill from Group-level outcomes into the actual parts of the organisation where performance, constraint and transformation occur.

The Blueprint MUST support this drill-down:

```text
Group outcome
→ Segment
→ Business unit
→ Product, service or customer group
→ Value stream
→ Process
→ Organisation and workforce
→ Technology platform
→ Data
→ Supplier or contract
→ Operating KPI
→ Revenue, cost, cash or risk consequence
```

#### Business-unit anatomy

Every material business unit SHOULD contain:

**Identity and role**

- purpose within the enterprise;
- products and services;
- customer segments;
- channels and geographies;
- regulatory perimeter;
- strategic role;
- dependencies on other units.

**Economics**

- revenue and revenue drivers;
- margin or contribution;
- direct and allocated cost;
- workforce cost;
- technology and supplier cost;
- capex;
- cash contribution;
- growth or decline;
- contribution to Group guidance.

**Leadership and organisation**

- accountable executive;
- operating leadership;
- decision rights;
- central versus local control;
- organisational layers;
- recent restructuring;
- critical vacancies;
- incentives;
- likely sponsors and blockers.

**Workforce**

- employees and contractors;
- role families;
- frontline versus corporate mix;
- location strategy;
- skills;
- productivity;
- attrition and hiring;
- union constraints;
- automation exposure;
- capability gaps.

**Processes and value streams**

- lead generation;
- sales;
- onboarding;
- fulfilment;
- service;
- billing;
- collections;
- complaints;
- field operations;
- asset operations;
- shared services;
- control and compliance;
- technology delivery.

For every material process, assess where Evidence permits:

- volume;
- unit cost;
- cycle time;
- quality or failure rate;
- rework;
- manual effort;
- decision points;
- customer impact;
- workforce ownership;
- platform and data dependency;
- supplier dependency;
- existing improvement programme.

**Technology and data**

For every material platform or domain, capture:

- capability supported;
- business units served;
- owner;
- vendor;
- age or modernity;
- integrations;
- data dependencies;
- resilience and cyber exposure;
- cost;
- current programme;
- contract or supplier;
- constraint;
- replacement or consolidation trajectory;
- AI readiness.

**Suppliers and contracts**

Capture:

- strategic supplier;
- scope;
- affected units and processes;
- contract term where known;
- value where evidenced;
- delivery performance;
- switching difficulty;
- incumbent influence;
- procurement route;
- renewal or expiry signal.

**Issue and opportunity mechanics**

An issue or opportunity MUST identify:

- specific business unit or cross-unit dependency;
- economic or operational mechanism;
- people, process, platform, data or supplier cause;
- measurable consequence;
- likely owner;
- existing response;
- evidence and Unknowns.

Generic statements such as “use AI to improve customer service” are non-compliant.

**Mandatory Level 3 artefacts:**

1. enterprise organisational map;
2. business-unit anatomy cards;
3. business-unit economics and value-pool view;
4. workforce and capability map;
5. value-stream and process map;
6. technology and data platform map;
7. supplier and contract map;
8. cross-unit dependency map;
9. issue and opportunity heat map;
10. financial-to-operating causal traces.

### Level 5 — Change dynamics and next financial release

**Purpose:** turn the static reconstruction into a forward model.

For every material metric or KPI, build a driver bridge:

```text
Current outcome
+ customer movement
+ volume
+ price
+ mix
+ productivity
+ programme benefits
+ cost inflation
+ workforce movement
+ supplier movement
+ capital and depreciation
+ regulation
+ macro conditions
= expected next-period range
```

The Blueprint MUST distinguish:

- reported actual;
- management guidance;
- management target;
- external consensus where used;
- Twin baseline forecast;
- upside scenario;
- downside scenario;
- Unknown.

Every forecast object MUST include:

- metric or KPI;
- current value and period;
- accounting basis and scope;
- management guidance;
- baseline expectation;
- expected range or directional judgement;
- primary drivers;
- leading indicators;
- management interventions;
- upside conditions;
- downside conditions;
- contradictory evidence;
- confidence;
- update triggers.

The model MUST avoid false precision.

The Blueprint SHOULD identify the likely management narrative at the next release, including what management will want to defend, explain, celebrate or reframe.

**Mandatory Level 5 artefacts:**

1. next-release driver bridge;
2. baseline, upside and downside scenarios;
3. leading-indicator register;
4. management promise tracker;
5. programme-benefit tracker;
6. forecast contradiction register;
7. expected management narrative;
8. update trigger list.

### Level 6 — Flagship transformation intelligence

**Purpose:** identify material AI-enabled enterprise reinvention only after the enterprise mechanics are sufficiently understood.

Every flagship transformation candidate MUST be anchored to:

- ownership or external pressure;
- board agenda;
- burning platform or strategic bet;
- material business unit;
- value pool;
- process or operating-model constraint;
- workforce, platform, data and supplier dependencies;
- measurable enterprise outcomes;
- likely executive ownership;
- procurement and incumbent reality;
- next-release or medium-term relevance.

#### Transformation object

Each candidate MUST contain:

1. **Enterprise problem** — the precise constraint or ambition.
2. **Causal chain** — how the condition creates financial, customer, operational or strategic consequence.
3. **Transformation proposition** — the operating change required.
4. **Specific AI role** — predict, detect, decide, optimise, generate, orchestrate, automate, assist or learn.
5. **Non-AI dependencies** — process, data, integration, operating model, workforce, controls and leadership.
6. **Enterprise scale** — affected business units, workforce, customers, platforms and value pools.
7. **Why now** — evidence-backed timing.
8. **Likely ownership** — named executive where evidenced, otherwise inferred role.
9. **Existing initiatives and incumbents** — what is already funded, contracted or in delivery.
10. **Value mechanisms** — revenue, margin, productivity, cash, capital, risk, resilience or customer impact.
11. **Evidence and Signals** — inspectable lineage.
12. **Contradictions and blockers** — why the proposition may be wrong or inaccessible.
13. **Unknowns** — information required for stronger conviction.
14. **Validation questions** — questions that strengthen or falsify the proposition.
15. **Next learning action** — research, relationship mapping, executive validation, proposition shaping or monitoring.
16. **Future reporting consequence** — what would appear differently in later financial or operating reporting if the transformation succeeded.

A flagship transformation MUST NOT be accepted merely because it is technologically plausible.

**Mandatory Level 6 artefacts:**

1. transformation thesis register;
2. flagship AI transformation blueprints;
3. executive ownership map;
4. incumbent and accessibility assessment;
5. value mechanism map;
6. validation and falsification plan;
7. next-action plan.

## 8. Evidence and truth contract

Every material Twin item MUST use one of these classes:

| Class | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Reported fact | Directly stated by an attributable source |
| Calculated fact | Transparently derived from reported values |
| Corroborated inference | Supported by multiple independent evidence families |
| Single-source inference | Plausible but weakly supported |
| Human-supplied knowledge | Supplied by a contributor and dated |
| Signal | Explanation of why an observed pattern may matter |
| Hypothesis | Proposition requiring validation or falsification |
| Unknown | Material information not established |
| Contradiction | Conflicting evidence or interpretations |
| Forecast | Time-bound expectation, not fact |
| Recommendation | Proposed action with inspectable lineage |

Every material item MUST include:

- item identifier;
- class;
- statement;
- enterprise domain;
- affected entity or entities;
- observed or effective date;
- collection date;
- source or contributor;
- source tier where available;
- supporting Evidence;
- contrary Evidence;
- confidence;
- freshness;
- contradiction state;
- explanation;
- status;
- next validation action where applicable.

No strong conclusion may rely on a citation that does not support the actual claim.

## 14. Acceptance criteria

A Blueprint complies with this Contract only when it can answer:

1. Who owns, controls and influences the enterprise?
2. Which agendas shape strategy, risk and capital allocation?
3. What must the board defend, fix, deliver and prove?
4. Which issues are genuine burning platforms?
5. Which hopes and bets management is relying on?
6. How does the enterprise generate the substantial majority of its revenue?
7. How are material costs structured and controlled?
8. Why does profit convert, or fail to convert, into cash?
9. Which business units create or destroy value?
10. How do processes, people, platforms, data and suppliers produce those outcomes?
11. Where are failure, delay, rework, duplication, leakage and constraint occurring?
12. What stops the enterprise from changing effectively?
13. Which leading indicators will shape the next financial release?
14. What are the baseline, upside and downside outcomes?
15. What is management likely to say at the next release?
16. Which flagship AI transformations are supported by enterprise mechanics?
17. Which executives are likely to own or resist them?
18. What is already being delivered and by whom?
19. Is the transformation commercially accessible?
20. What Evidence supports each conclusion?
21. What contradicts it?
22. What remains Unknown?
23. What should be learned next?
24. Has the Blueprint materially changed the strategic user's understanding?

The Blueprint MUST fail acceptance where it:

- merely summarises reporting;
- uses generic transformation language;
- presents inference as fact;
- cannot drill below Group or segment level;
- lacks business-unit economics;
- ignores existing programmes or incumbents;
- invents precision;
- hides contradictions;
- claims opportunity without accessibility;
- provides Recommendations without lineage;
- substitutes confident prose for missing Evidence.

## 15. Review and quality gates

### Gate 1 — Scope integrity

- enterprise boundary is explicit;
- reporting and source cut-off dates are explicit;
- exclusions are visible.

### Gate 2 — Evidence integrity

- material claims have support;
- source quality is assessed;
- calculations are reproducible;
- human knowledge is labelled.

### Gate 3 — Depth integrity

- key conclusions reach the deepest evidence-supported operating layer;
- business-unit, process, workforce, technology and supplier mechanics are connected;
- coverage gaps are visible.

### Gate 4 — Reasoning integrity

- facts, Signals, Hypotheses, forecasts and Recommendations are separated;
- alternatives and contradictions are preserved;
- causal chains are inspectable.

### Gate 5 — Predictive integrity

- forecasts are driver-based;
- leading indicators are defined;
- ranges and scenarios replace false precision;
- update triggers are explicit.

### Gate 6 — Transformation integrity

- propositions are enterprise-specific;
- AI has a defined role;
- non-AI dependencies are explicit;
- ownership, incumbency and accessibility are considered;
- validation questions can falsify the thesis.

### Gate 7 — Commercial usefulness

- the output changes understanding, timing, qualification, executive engagement or next action;
- the next action is proportionate to conviction.

## 16. Refresh and learning contract

A Blueprint MUST define:

- normal refresh cadence;
- event-driven refresh triggers;
- volatile attributes requiring more frequent review;
- forecasts that will be scored against actual results;
- Hypotheses to strengthen, weaken, retire or split;
- stale Evidence;
- changed business-unit or executive ownership;
- new programmes, suppliers or contracts;
- lessons from commercial outcomes.

A refreshed Blueprint SHOULD show:

- what changed;
- why it changed;
- which prior conclusions strengthened or weakened;
- which forecasts were accurate;
- which assumptions failed;
- which new Unknowns emerged;
- what should be done differently.

- validate imported Blueprint completeness;
- render the Twin and its coverage;
- preserve Evidence and lineage;
- maintain versions;
- compare refreshes;
- manage Hypothesis lifecycle;
- expose Unknowns and Contradictions;
- score predictive accuracy;
- support human acceptance;
- update accepted Observations and Enterprise Model state through governed processes.

Flora MUST NOT treat a completed Blueprint report as canonical Enterprise Model state without the required acceptance and lineage processes.

The Markdown Contract is the canonical source. The Research Agent Guide is a derived operational companion. Where they diverge, this Contract wins.

## 18. Change control

Enduring changes to:

- Commercial Digital Twin meaning;
- canonical Enterprise Model objects;
- Observation doctrine;
- evidence lineage;
- Hypothesis lifecycle;
- acceptance of provider output;
- recommendation thresholds;

MUST be reconciled with the owning architecture paper or an Accepted ADR.

Editorial changes, examples and research workflow improvements MAY update this Contract without creating an ADR, provided they do not alter authoritative architecture.

## Appendix A — Minimal transformation causal trace

```text
Board pressure:
Customer service and cost credibility must improve.

Business unit:
Named operating unit or cross-enterprise function.

Economic mechanism:
High cost-to-serve and avoidable customer loss.

Operating mechanism:
Manual diagnosis, fragmented data and repeated hand-offs.

Technology and data:
Named service, workflow, customer and data platforms.

Workforce:
Named roles and decision points.

Leading indicator:
Complaints, repeat contacts, cycle time, churn or unit cost.

Expected financial consequence:
Lower service cost, reduced churn and improved cash or margin.

Transformation:
AI-enabled service orchestration and decision support.

Dependencies:
Data quality, process redesign, controls, workforce adoption and platform integration.

Unknowns:
Sponsor, existing supplier, budget, baseline economics and procurement route.

Next action:
Validate operating baseline and ownership before shaping a proposition.
```

## Appendix B — Research completion statement

Every Blueprint MUST close with:

- **Depth achieved:** levels and domains reached.
- **Evidence coverage:** strong, partial and weak areas.
- **Most important Unknowns:** ranked by decision impact.
- **Most important Contradictions:** unresolved competing explanations.
- **Forecast confidence:** baseline, upside and downside.
- **Transformation confidence:** strongest and weakest theses.
- **Commercial accessibility:** known, inferred or Unknown.
- **Recommended next learning actions:** ordered by value of information.
- **Canonical state:** candidate, reviewed, accepted or rejected.
