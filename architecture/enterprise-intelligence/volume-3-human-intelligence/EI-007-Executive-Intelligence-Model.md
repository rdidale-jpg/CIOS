# EI-007 — Executive Intelligence Model

**Purpose:** Define how CIOS models people, leadership and influence.  
**Status:** Draft  
**Owner:** Rob / CIOS  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-03

## Architectural position

Enterprise Intelligence defines what CIOS knows about an enterprise. CIRM defines how CIOS reasons over evidence and signals. Flora is the first runtime implementation that combines both into operational intelligence.

## Purpose

Define how CIOS models people, leadership and influence. This paper is documentation-only and avoids runtime implementation detail.

## Why executive intelligence matters

Why executive intelligence matters describes the architectural expectations for this part of the Enterprise Intelligence model. It should be evidence-linked, freshness-aware and distinct from CIRM process mechanics. CIRM explains how CIOS reasons; this section defines what CIOS should know about the enterprise.

## Ethical and compliance boundaries

Only public, permissible and compliant sources should be used. Executive Intelligence must respect privacy, source terms, applicable law and CIOS governance. Sensitive personal inference, intrusive profiling and non-public collection are outside the intended scope.
