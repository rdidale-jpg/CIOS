# EI-009 — Transformation Pressure Model

**Purpose:** Define internal and external pressures that make transformation more likely.  
**Status:** Draft  
**Owner:** Rob / CIOS  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-03

## Architectural position

Enterprise Intelligence defines what CIOS knows about an enterprise. CIRM defines how CIOS reasons over evidence and signals. Flora is the first runtime implementation that combines both into operational intelligence.

## Purpose

Define internal and external pressures that make transformation more likely. This paper is documentation-only and avoids runtime implementation detail.

## Pressure taxonomy

Pressure taxonomy describes the architectural expectations for this part of the Enterprise Intelligence model. It should be evidence-linked, freshness-aware and distinct from CIRM process mechanics. CIRM explains how CIOS reasons; this section defines what CIOS should know about the enterprise.

## Internal pressure

- Legacy technology
- Operating cost
- Service quality
- Cyber exposure
- Workforce constraints
- Fragmented data
- Delivery failure
- Technical debt
- Low margin
- Debt burden
- Underperforming business unit

## External pressure

- Regulation
- Political direction
- Customer expectations
- Market disruption
- Competitor moves
- Supplier change
- Economic pressure
- Technology shifts
- AI-first entrant pressure
