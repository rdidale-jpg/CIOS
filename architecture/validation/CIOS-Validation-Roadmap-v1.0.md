**Status:** Review  
**Authority:** Programme governance / validation roadmap

**Production behaviour:** Documentation only. This roadmap does not change runtime behaviour, production Researcher packs, production Assurance packs, accepted architecture authority or release-profile membership.

## Mission

Establish the formal validation programme for Review-stage Commercial Digital Twin architecture before any artefact is promoted to Accepted.

The roadmap governs validation for:

- EIF-001 — Enterprise Intelligence Foundation Model;
- EOD-001 — Enterprise Opportunity Discovery Standard;
- OT-001 — Opportunity Twin Specification;
- RTP-001 — Research-to-Positioning Input Contract;
- OPI-001 — Opportunity Positioning Intelligence;
- the MOD reference enterprise evidence already completed;
- planned VodafoneThree, National Grid and United Utilities validation enterprises;
- founder validation scoring;
- promotion rules;
- Phase 2 exit criteria.

## Accepted foundation

Validation must be performed against the accepted CIOS architecture foundation, including the accepted Reference Architecture, accepted ADRs, accepted Founding Papers, accepted Enterprise Intelligence ownership papers, AP-001, AP-002, RP-001, RP-002, the Architecture Authority Registry and the Document Map.

The accepted foundation supplies:

1. authority boundaries;
2. evidence and lineage discipline;
3. Observation and Enterprise Model semantics;
4. Commercial Digital Twin memory boundaries;
5. Researcher and Assurance role constraints;
6. compilation and profile-membership rules;
7. non-promotion controls for Review material.

## Review artefacts requiring validation

### EIF-001 — Enterprise Intelligence Foundation Model

EIF-001 must be validated as a durable method for building initial enterprise understanding before opportunity discovery. Validation must test whether it produces coherent enterprise identity, operating context, change landscape, risk, technology, ecosystem, Unknowns, Contradictions and stable inputs for EOD-001.

### EOD-001 — Enterprise Opportunity Discovery Standard

EOD-001 must be validated as the bridge from enterprise understanding to opportunity portfolio discovery. Validation must test whether it discovers commercially significant opportunities without starting from a presumed procurement, preserves Unknowns and Contradictions, and separates evidence, interpretation, projection and commercial judgement.

### OT-001 — Opportunity Twin Specification

OT-001 must be validated as a governed Opportunity Twin interface beneath EI-006. Validation must test whether it keeps opportunity state inspectable, preserves lineage, receives RTP-001 handover material safely, and treats OPI-001 positioning objects as candidate material until separately validated.

### RTP-001 — Research-to-Positioning Input Contract

RTP-001 must be validated as the governed handover from Research to Positioning. Validation must test whether evidence, Unknowns, Contradictions, caveats, decision envelopes and unsupported claims are preserved when research outputs are consumed by positioning work.

### OPI-001 — Opportunity Positioning Intelligence

OPI-001 must be validated as a method for creating candidate positioning intelligence without over-claiming. Validation must test whether narratives, value hypotheses, buyer-problem framing, differentiation angles and evidence demands remain candidate objects until validated.

## Reference Enterprise 001 — MOD

MOD is recorded as completed reference-enterprise evidence.

MOD evidence demonstrates that CIOS can learn from a bounded, evidence-preserving Commercial Digital Twin assurance review. It is valuable evidence for architecture learning, package discipline, uncertainty preservation, decision safety and non-promotion governance.

MOD is not universal proof. It does not demonstrate cross-industry validation by itself. It must not be used to promote Review artefacts to Accepted without materially different enterprise evidence.

## Reference Enterprise 002 — VodafoneThree

VodafoneThree is a planned validation enterprise.

Its validation role is to test CIOS in a telecommunications context with complex integration, network, customer, regulatory, operating-model and commercial-change dynamics. The validation outcome is not pre-judged and must be evidence-led.

Expected focus:

- EIF-001 enterprise foundation completeness;
- EOD-001 opportunity portfolio discovery from enterprise-change evidence;
- evidence freshness and contradiction handling;
- ability to distinguish enterprise need from provider-specific opportunity claims.

## Reference Enterprise 003 — National Grid

National Grid is a planned validation enterprise.

Its validation role is to test CIOS in a regulated energy and critical-infrastructure context with long-horizon asset, system resilience, market-transition, security and public-interest constraints. The validation outcome is not pre-judged and must be evidence-led.

Expected focus:

- enterprise foundation durability under regulated infrastructure complexity;
- opportunity discovery across asset, grid, digital, operational and transformation programmes;
- evidence boundary controls for public-domain and sensitive-system claims;
- commercial value without unsupported provider-fit inference.

## Reference Enterprise 004 — United Utilities

United Utilities is a planned validation enterprise.

Its validation role is to test CIOS in a regulated water utilities context with operational resilience, environmental obligations, asset investment, customer outcomes, field operations and regulatory performance dynamics. The validation outcome is not pre-judged and must be evidence-led.

Expected focus:

- foundation coverage for regulated utility operating models;
- opportunity discovery across operational, environmental, customer and capital-delivery domains;
- practical value for prioritising evidence demands;
- positioning discipline where public need does not prove supplier accessibility.

## Promotion rules

No Review artefact may become Accepted unless it demonstrates all of the following:

1. **Architectural coherence** — the artefact fits the accepted CIOS foundation, preserves owning-paper boundaries and does not conflict with AP-001, AP-002, RP-001, RP-002, the Authority Registry, the Document Map or accepted ADRs.
2. **Practical value** — the artefact helps users produce, inspect, govern or improve Commercial Digital Twin work in a repeatable way.
3. **Commercial value** — the artefact improves commercial reasoning, opportunity understanding, evidence demand, positioning discipline or decision safety without unsupported provider-specific claims.
4. **Evidence from materially different enterprises** — the artefact is validated across MOD plus planned validation enterprises or an equivalent governance-approved set that is materially different by sector, operating model and commercial context.
5. **Founder validation score of at least 20 / 24** — the founder scorecard must reach at least 20 before promotion is considered.
6. **No authority shortcut** — completion of the roadmap does not automatically promote any artefact. Promotion requires a separate accepted architecture decision and registry update.
7. **No production-pack shortcut** — Accepted promotion, if later approved, must separately decide whether any release-profile membership changes are appropriate.

## Founder validation scorecard

The founder scorecard is a governance aid, not automatic approval. The target score is at least **20 / 24** before promotion is considered.

| Dimension | Maximum score | Scoring question |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Architectural coherence | 4 | Does the artefact fit the accepted CIOS architecture without creating conflicting authority? |
| Evidence discipline | 4 | Does it preserve lineage, Unknowns, Contradictions, caveats and decision envelopes? |
| Practical utility | 4 | Does it help real users complete validation, research, assurance or positioning work? |
| Commercial utility | 4 | Does it improve commercial reasoning without unsupported provider-fit or pursuit claims? |
| Cross-enterprise robustness | 4 | Does it hold across materially different enterprises, not only MOD? |
| Governance safety | 4 | Does it avoid runtime changes, pack promotion, authority drift and implied acceptance? |
| **Total** | **24** | **Promotion consideration threshold: 20 / 24 or higher.** |

## Phase 2 exit criteria

Phase 2 exits only when the validation programme can show all of the following:

1. MOD completed evidence is recorded accurately as reference-enterprise evidence, not universal proof.
2. VodafoneThree validation is completed or explicitly deferred by governance with reasons and replacement coverage.
3. National Grid validation is completed or explicitly deferred by governance with reasons and replacement coverage.
4. United Utilities validation is completed or explicitly deferred by governance with reasons and replacement coverage.
5. EIF-001, EOD-001, OT-001, RTP-001 and OPI-001 each have an evidence-backed validation summary.
6. All Review artefact statuses remain accurate until a separate accepted decision changes them.
7. The founder scorecard has been completed for every candidate promotion and reaches at least 20 / 24 before promotion is considered.
8. No unsupported claim of cross-industry validation is made.
9. No production Researcher or Assurance pack has been changed by validation-roadmap work.
10. Promotion recommendations, if any, are separated from promotion decisions.

## Governance and evidence requirements

Each validation enterprise must produce a validation record that includes:

- enterprise boundary and effective date;
- evidence sources inspected;
- artefacts tested;
- findings by artefact;
- evidence of architectural coherence;
- evidence of practical value;
- evidence of commercial value;
- Unknowns, Contradictions and caveats preserved;
- unsupported claims rejected or deferred;
- founder scorecard result;
- explicit recommendation: remain Review, revise, retire or consider promotion;
- statement that the validation record itself does not promote architecture.

Evidence must remain materially traceable. Where validation relies on public-domain evidence, public-source limitations must be explicit. Where validation uses human-supplied knowledge, it must be labelled, bounded and dated. Where claims require internal validation, they must not be treated as externally proven.
