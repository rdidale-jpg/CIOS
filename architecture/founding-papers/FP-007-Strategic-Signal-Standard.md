# FP-007 — Strategic Signal Standard

**Purpose:** Define the Strategic Signal as the fundamental intelligence object within CIOS.
**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** Rob / CIOS
**Last updated:** 2026-07-02

## Relationship to the CIOS Intelligence Reference Model

The CIOS Intelligence Reference Model (CIRM) defines how CIOS converts observable enterprise reality into strategic commercial judgement. Its canonical pipeline is:

```text
Observable Enterprise Reality
→ Governed Source Collection
→ Raw Evidence
→ Evidence Quality Assessment
→ Strategic Signals
→ Commercial Insights
→ Transformation Themes
→ Transformation Theses
→ Hypothesis Validation
→ Commercial Conviction
→ Executive Recommendations
→ Commercial Outcomes
→ Continuous Learning
```

FP-007 governs Strategic Signals: the point where Raw Evidence and Evidence Quality Assessment become commercially meaningful interpretation.

## Transformation Pressure and Transformation Inevitability

**Transformation Pressure** is the internal and external forces that make change more likely or necessary. Internal pressure may include legacy technology, operating cost, service quality, cyber exposure, workforce constraints, fragmented data, delivery failure and technical debt. External pressure may include regulation, political direction, customer expectations, market disruption, competitive moves, supplier change, economic pressure and technology shifts.

**Transformation Inevitability** is the degree to which an enterprise appears structurally compelled to transform, regardless of whether procurement, budget or sponsor evidence is visible. Transformation inevitability is not the same as commercial opportunity: an enterprise may need to transform but still be inaccessible, already committed to another provider, internally constrained or commercially unattractive.

## Inspectable Reasoning Lineage

No recommendation may exist unless its reasoning chain can be inspected:

```text
Executive Recommendation
→ Commercial Conviction
→ Hypothesis / Transformation Thesis
→ Commercial Insight
→ Strategic Signal
→ Raw Evidence
→ Source
```

If any link is missing, the recommendation should be downgraded to a learning action or evidence demand.

## 1. Purpose

This paper defines the Strategic Signal as the fundamental intelligence object within CIOS and the reasoning bridge between governed evidence and commercial judgement.

Evidence records observable facts: what was said, published, awarded, changed, measured or observed. Strategic Signals explain why those facts may matter commercially. A signal is not a recommendation, not a pursuit decision and not a prediction. It is an explainable interpretation of evidence that helps Flora reason about enterprise transformation before a formal opportunity exists.

The core reasoning chain is:

```text
Raw Evidence
→ Strategic Signal
→ Commercial Insight
→ Transformation Theme
→ Transformation Thesis
→ Hypothesis Validation
→ Executive Recommendation
```

Signals are therefore the first point at which CIOS moves from observation to meaning. They must remain grounded, inspectable and capable of weakening when the evidence beneath them weakens.

## 2. Why Signals Exist

Enterprises rarely announce buying intent in a clean, complete form. They emit fragments: leadership changes, budget pressure, platform investments, regulatory obligations, procurement activity, supplier movement, operating failures and strategic language. Individually, these fragments are often insufficient. Together, they may reveal commercial timing, transformation pressure or executive priority.

Strategic Signals exist to prevent Flora from jumping directly from facts to recommendations. They give CIOS a disciplined middle layer where evidence can be interpreted without being overstated.

Signals help Flora:

- distinguish facts from commercial interpretation;
- connect evidence across source categories;
- expose the reasoning behind commercial meaning;
- identify unknowns and contradictions early;
- track whether a commercial pattern is strengthening or weakening;
- support later hypotheses and recommendations without hiding provenance.

A signal answers the question:

> Given the evidence we can inspect, what commercially relevant change may be forming, and how strongly should we believe that interpretation?

## 3. Relationship to Evidence

Evidence is the foundation of the signal model. Evidence records must remain factual, attributable and source-specific. Signals may interpret evidence, but they must not mutate evidence into opinion.

The relationship is deliberately asymmetric:

- evidence can exist without a signal;
- a signal must not exist without evidence;
- a signal may reference multiple evidence records;
- one evidence record may contribute to multiple signals;
- contradictory evidence must be preserved rather than discarded;
- unknowns must remain visible rather than being filled by fluent inference.

```text
Observable Fact
→ Evidence Record
→ Evidence Confidence
→ Strategic Signal
→ Signal Strength
```

Evidence confidence describes how reliable the observed fact appears to be. Signal strength describes how strongly the evidence supports the commercial interpretation. These are related but not identical. A highly reliable fact may still support only a weak commercial signal if its meaning is ambiguous.

## 4. Signal Anatomy

Every Strategic Signal should include the following elements.

### Observation

The concise statement of what has been observed. The observation should be factual and should not contain unsupported commercial claims.

### Evidence References

The specific evidence records that support the signal, including source lineage where available. References should allow a human reviewer to inspect the basis of the signal.

### Commercial Meaning

The interpretation of why the observation may matter commercially. This should be framed as a reasoned judgement, not a certainty.

### Transformation Dimension

The transformation area implicated by the signal, such as strategy, finance, technology, procurement, leadership, operations, regulation, delivery or suppliers.

### Enterprise Impact

The likely area of enterprise consequence: cost, growth, resilience, service quality, customer experience, compliance, operating model, workforce, platform modernisation or competitive position.

### Supporting Evidence

Evidence that strengthens the signal or corroborates its meaning.

### Contradictory Evidence

Evidence that weakens, challenges or complicates the signal. Contradiction is an intelligence asset because it prevents premature conviction.

### Unknowns

Material questions that remain unanswered. Unknowns should be explicit, because they determine the next evidence needed.

### Commercial Importance

The significance of the signal if true. Importance is not the same as confidence. A weakly evidenced signal may still be commercially important if it concerns an executive priority, major budget, regulatory obligation or urgent transformation pressure.

### Evidence Confidence

Assessment of the reliability, specificity, authority and freshness of the underlying evidence.

### Signal Strength

Assessment of how strongly the evidence supports the commercial interpretation. Signal strength should be lower where evidence is thin, ambiguous, old, dependent on one source or contradicted.

### Freshness

Assessment of whether the signal reflects current enterprise reality. A signal based on aged evidence should decay unless refreshed or corroborated.

### Momentum

Assessment of whether the signal is accelerating, stable, slowing or dormant. Momentum should reflect change over time rather than static importance.

### Recommended Next Evidence

The next evidence that would most improve judgement. This may include named executive confirmation, procurement evidence, budget visibility, delivery milestones, supplier movement, regulatory updates or contradiction checks.

## 5. Signal Lifecycle

Strategic Signals evolve as evidence changes.

### New

A signal has been created from initial evidence but has limited corroboration or history.

### Emerging

Additional evidence suggests the signal may represent a meaningful commercial pattern, but unknowns remain material.

### Strengthening

Independent or higher-quality evidence increases confidence in the interpretation. Contradictions may reduce or coverage may improve.

### Stable

The signal remains supported, current and commercially relevant, but is not materially changing.

### Weakening

Evidence ages, contradictions appear, expected corroboration fails to emerge or enterprise conditions change.

### Dormant

The signal is not disproven, but there is insufficient recent movement to treat it as active.

### Retired

The signal is no longer useful for commercial reasoning because it has been disproven, superseded, completed or made irrelevant by enterprise change.

## 6. Signal Corroboration

Signals become more reliable when corroborated through independent evidence.

### Single-source

A signal is supported by one evidence source. Single-source signals can be useful but should usually remain low strength unless the source is highly authoritative and the claim is narrow.

### Multi-source

A signal is supported by more than one source. This improves confidence when the sources are not merely repeating the same original claim.

### Multi-category

A signal is supported across evidence categories, such as strategy plus procurement, leadership plus budget, or regulation plus delivery.

### Cross-sector

A signal is strengthened by comparable patterns across adjacent sectors or peer organisations. Cross-sector corroboration should support context, not replace enterprise-specific evidence.

### Independent confirmation

A signal is strongest when independent evidence confirms the same commercial meaning without relying on the same originating source. Executive statements, procurement records, regulatory findings and delivery evidence can provide especially strong confirmation when aligned.

## 7. Signal Clustering

Individual signals are useful, but CIOS becomes more powerful when signals cluster.

Multiple signals combine into:

### Commercial Insights

A commercial insight explains a near-term implication for an enterprise: a likely pressure, gap, priority, risk or opening that may merit executive attention.

### Transformation Themes

A transformation theme groups related insights into a recurring enterprise pattern, such as legacy platform modernisation, AI-enabled service redesign, data governance, resilience investment or cost transformation.

### Transformation Theses

A transformation thesis is a higher-order judgement about what may be happening in the enterprise, why it matters, what evidence supports it, what could disprove it and what action may be justified.

```text
Signal + Signal + Signal
→ Commercial Insight
→ Transformation Theme
→ Transformation Thesis
```

Clustering must not erase uncertainty. Each higher-order construct should preserve the signals, evidence, contradictions and unknowns beneath it.

## 8. Signal Governance

Strategic Signals are governed intelligence objects.

They should never contain unsupported commercial claims. If Flora cannot point to evidence, the claim should be marked as an unknown or hypothesis rather than stated as a signal.

Signals should remain explainable. A human reviewer should be able to inspect the observation, evidence references, interpretation, confidence, strength, contradictions and next evidence.

Signals should remain inspectable. Their reasoning should not be hidden inside opaque scoring, untraceable summaries or unlabelled model inference.

Signals should weaken when evidence weakens. Aging evidence, failed corroboration, leadership changes, strategy shifts or contradiction should reduce signal strength until refreshed or retired.

Signal governance protects CIOS from three failure modes:

1. treating evidence volume as intelligence quality;
2. treating plausible narrative as commercial truth;
3. treating recommendations as valid when the signal layer is weak.

## 9. Examples

The following examples are illustrative architecture patterns. They show how Flora should reason from evidence to signal to commercial meaning without claiming that any current opportunity exists.

### BT

```text
Evidence
→ BT publishes or updates material indicating network modernisation, cost transformation, digital service priorities or supplier ecosystem change.

Strategic Signal
→ BT may be concentrating transformation attention on modernising infrastructure, improving operating efficiency and shifting service delivery models.

Commercial Meaning
→ If corroborated by executive ownership, budget visibility and procurement movement, this may indicate a commercially relevant transformation environment for providers aligned to network, data, automation or operating-model change.
```

### DWP

```text
Evidence
→ DWP publishes evidence of service reform, digital delivery priorities, operational pressure, policy obligations or procurement activity linked to citizen services.

Strategic Signal
→ DWP may be experiencing transformation pressure around service scale, automation, data, resilience and delivery performance.

Commercial Meaning
→ If supported by leadership statements, delivery milestones and procurement evidence, this may justify executive engagement around AI-enabled service redesign, operational efficiency and responsible automation.
```

### National Grid

```text
Evidence
→ National Grid publishes evidence of energy transition investment, grid resilience requirements, regulatory obligations, infrastructure delivery or digital control capability.

Strategic Signal
→ National Grid may be facing a compounded transformation requirement across infrastructure capacity, resilience, regulation and digital operations.

Commercial Meaning
→ If corroborated by investment plans, regulatory filings, supplier activity and delivery evidence, this may indicate a strategic environment for providers with credible energy, data, AI, cyber and critical infrastructure transformation capability.
```

## 10. Open Questions

- What minimum evidence threshold should be required before a signal can influence recommendations?
- How should CIOS distinguish signal strength from commercial importance in user experience?
- What decay rules should apply by evidence category and sector?
- How should human analyst review change signal lifecycle state?
- When should contradictory evidence pause, weaken or retire a signal?
- How should Flora represent signals that are commercially important but ethically or operationally unsuitable to pursue?

This paper governs the first interpretation stage of CIRM. It defines how raw evidence, assessed through [FP-004](FP-004-Evidence-Acquisition-Standard.md) and [FP-006](FP-006-Source-Quality-Standard.md), becomes Strategic Signals without becoming unsupported recommendations.
