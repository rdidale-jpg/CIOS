# FP-008 — Commercial Conviction Model

**Purpose:** Define how Flora converts evidence into commercial confidence without confusing confidence with opportunity.
**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** Rob / CIOS
**Last updated:** 2026-07-02

## 1. Purpose

This paper defines the Commercial Conviction Model for CIOS. Commercial conviction is the structured assessment of whether sufficient evidence exists to justify executive engagement.

Commercial conviction is not a probability model. It does not claim to calculate the likelihood that an enterprise will buy, award work or select a provider. It instead asks whether Flora has enough governed evidence, signal strength, commercial relevance and unresolved unknowns to support confident human action.

The model exists to keep Flora from confusing available information with actionable judgement.

```text
Evidence
→ Signals
→ Hypotheses
→ Commercial Conviction
→ Executive Readiness
```

## 2. Why Confidence Is Not Enough

Confidence is necessary but insufficient. Flora may have high confidence in a fact while having low conviction that executive engagement is justified. Conversely, Flora may have limited evidence for a commercially important change that merits careful exploration but not yet a recommendation.

CIOS should distinguish five related concepts.

### Evidence Confidence

Evidence confidence describes how reliable an evidence record appears to be. It reflects source authority, specificity, freshness, attribution and consistency.

### Commercial Attractiveness

Commercial attractiveness describes how valuable, relevant or strategically interesting the potential enterprise situation may be if the interpretation is true. It reflects scale, urgency, fit, differentiation and potential impact.

### Commercial Conviction

Commercial conviction describes whether the available evidence and reasoning are sufficient to justify a level of commercial action. It is a judgement about readiness, not a forecast of win probability.

### Executive Readiness

Executive readiness describes whether the reasoning can withstand senior scrutiny. An executive-ready view should explain the evidence, the meaning, the unknowns, the contradictions and the recommended next action.

### Transformation Inevitability

Transformation inevitability describes whether the enterprise appears compelled to change because of structural pressure, regulation, cost, competition, customer need, technology debt or strategic commitment. Inevitable change may still be commercially inaccessible if fit, timing or procurement readiness is weak.

## 3. Conviction Components

Commercial conviction should be assessed across the following components.

### Evidence Quality

The authority, specificity, freshness and reliability of the evidence foundation.

### Evidence Coverage

The breadth of evidence across relevant categories such as strategy, finance, technology, procurement, leadership, operations, regulation, delivery and suppliers.

### Corroboration

The degree to which independent sources, categories or events support the same commercial interpretation.

### Commercial Importance

The significance of the potential issue or transformation if the interpretation is true.

### Executive Ownership

Evidence that named leaders, accountable executives or board-level stakeholders own the priority.

### Budget Visibility

Evidence of allocated funding, cost pressure, capital investment, spending plans, efficiency targets or financial capacity.

### Transformation Pressure

Evidence that the enterprise must change because of operational, regulatory, market, customer, technology or resilience pressure.

### Competitive Position

Evidence that the enterprise is responding to peers, market disruption, competitive threats or strategic differentiation needs.

### Provider Fit

Assessment of whether the provider has credible relevance to the enterprise's apparent transformation need. Fit should be grounded in capabilities, sector credibility, relationships, delivery evidence and strategic alignment.

### Procurement Readiness

Evidence of tendering, framework access, contract expiry, supplier review, procurement pipeline, buying mechanism or commercial route.

### Unknowns

Material missing information that could change the judgement, such as absence of budget, sponsor, timing, decision route or incumbent supplier position.

### Contradictions

Evidence that challenges the commercial interpretation, including deprioritisation, budget cuts, leadership exits, cancelled programmes, strategic reversal or incompatible procurement evidence.

## 4. Conviction Levels

Conviction levels are qualitative bands designed to support explainable judgement.

### Very Low

The evidence base is weak, stale, generic or speculative. Commercial action would be premature.

### Low

Some relevant evidence exists, but coverage, corroboration or commercial meaning is insufficient. Flora should prioritise learning rather than engagement.

### Emerging

A plausible commercial pattern is forming. Evidence supports exploration, but unknowns remain material and contradictions may be unresolved.

### Medium

Evidence quality, coverage and corroboration are sufficient to justify structured commercial review or targeted discovery. The view is not yet fully executive-ready.

### Strong

The reasoning is well supported across multiple components. Executive engagement may be justified if provider fit, timing and governance are acceptable.

### Very Strong

The evidence base is robust, current, corroborated and commercially important. Unknowns are limited or explicitly bounded. The judgement is suitable for senior action, while still remaining falsifiable.

## 5. Conviction Evolution

Commercial conviction changes over time. It is not a permanent label.

Conviction strengthens when stronger evidence appears, coverage improves, independent corroboration increases, contradictions reduce, named ownership emerges, budget becomes visible or procurement movement appears.

Conviction weakens when evidence ages, expected evidence fails to appear, contradictions grow, leadership changes, strategy shifts, budget disappears, delivery stalls or provider fit becomes less credible.

Conviction accelerates when multiple independent events converge quickly, such as a new executive appointment, formal strategy update, budget commitment and procurement notice.

Conviction decays when no fresh evidence appears in a context where movement would be expected. Decay should be visible so that old confidence is not mistaken for current judgement.

## 6. Conviction versus Opportunity

High conviction does not necessarily mean high opportunity. Low conviction does not necessarily mean low opportunity.

A high-conviction case may show that an enterprise is definitely transforming, but the commercial opportunity may be poor because the work is already awarded, the provider fit is weak, procurement is closed, the incumbent position is locked or the issue is outside the provider's mandate.

A low-conviction case may still be commercially attractive because early evidence points to a large, urgent or strategically important transformation, but Flora lacks enough corroboration to justify executive engagement. In that case the right action is targeted evidence acquisition, not dismissal.

Examples:

- A utility may have high conviction around grid resilience investment but low accessible opportunity if procurement routes are closed and incumbents dominate delivery.
- A government department may have low conviction around an emerging automation theme but high potential attractiveness if service pressure, policy urgency and scale are significant.
- A telecommunications provider may show strong transformation inevitability but weak provider fit if the identified need sits outside the provider's credible capability.

## 7. Conviction Dashboard

A future CIOS visual should separate related judgements rather than collapse them into one opaque score.

The dashboard should show:

- Evidence Confidence;
- Commercial Attractiveness;
- Conviction;
- Momentum;
- Unknowns.

A useful visual might show conviction as an executive-readiness band, evidence confidence as a foundation indicator, attractiveness as a commercial scale indicator, momentum as direction of travel, and unknowns as explicit blockers.

```text
Evidence Confidence | Commercial Attractiveness | Conviction | Momentum | Unknowns
        High         |          Medium           |   Emerging |    ↑     | Budget, sponsor
```

The purpose of the dashboard is not to create false precision. It is to make judgement inspectable.

## 8. Human Judgement

Human judgement supplements the model. It does not replace it, and the model does not replace human accountability.

### Analyst Override

An analyst may override or qualify a conviction level when contextual knowledge, ethical considerations, relationship intelligence or known market conditions are not yet represented in evidence. Overrides should be explicit, reasoned and reviewable.

### Future Rob Score

A future Rob Score may represent Rob's commercial judgement as an expert calibration layer. It should be treated as structured feedback into Flora's learning loop, not as an unchallengeable truth.

### Executive Feedback

Executive feedback should help Flora learn which evidence patterns were persuasive, which unknowns mattered, which recommendations were useful and which assumptions failed. Feedback should improve future conviction assessment while preserving the original evidence trail.

Human judgement is strongest when it is transparent about why it agrees with, strengthens, weakens or overrides the model.
