## 4. Opportunity Completeness Review

### 4.1 Enterprise-led generation

Enterprise-led generation should begin with MOD mission pressures, operating constraints, governance, readiness, affordability, digital/AI adoption, supplier resilience and change-delivery bottlenecks. On that basis, the current candidate universe appears directionally adequate for maintaining pressure-led hypotheses, but not complete enough to claim all material opportunities because internal priorities, classified constraints, budget ownership and receiving-owner routes are not validated.

### 4.2 Commercial-domain-led generation

Commercial-domain generation should test digital transformation, data platforms, AI enablement, cyber-adjacent controls where in scope, ERP/back-office modernisation, service management, managed services, systems integration, outsourcing, advisory and change services. The current understanding supports broad domain coverage as a search frame. It does not support ranking domains by attractiveness, provider fit or near-term accessibility.

### 4.3 Procurement and contract-led generation

Procurement-led generation must not infer opportunity from enterprise need. It should reconcile public notices, frameworks, incumbent contracts, recompetes, extensions, direct awards, lots, eligibility constraints and buyer units. The current understanding is insufficient for contract-led readiness because public commercial routes do not prove eligibility, access or workshare.

### 4.4 Continuity from prior MOD releases

Continuity can be accepted only for preserved governed records, not because a prior release asserted a theme. Candidate continuity should be checked for retained identifiers, changed evidence state, retired assumptions and newly opened unknowns. The current release can act as the baseline for continuity, but any refresh must identify what changed rather than regenerating a full narrative.

### 4.5 Completeness judgement

Opportunity candidate completeness is **not proven**. The candidate universe is sufficient for learning and validation planning if it keeps rejected, deferred and uncertain candidates visible. It is not sufficient for generic completeness, pursuit readiness or provider-specific prioritisation.

| Who owns CSM outcomes and budget? | Named accountable owner or governance body. | Unknown. |

## 7. Decision Envelope

### Supported now

The current MOD Enterprise Understanding can responsibly support:

1. MOD enterprise-priority learning decisions.
2. Bounded discussion of pressures, mechanisms and candidate reinvention seams.
3. Retained broader flagship sequencing as a hypothesis-management exercise.
4. Executive validation and account-learning decisions.
5. Targeted evidence-demand prioritisation.
6. Flora runtime ingestion/reconstruction testing using MOD v1.3 as a governed baseline.
7. Bounded incremental refresh planning without regenerating the complete release.

### Supported only with explicit caveats

1. Thematic digital, data, AI, technology-transformation, managed-service and outsourcing opportunity hypotheses.
2. Stakeholder-map hypotheses.
3. Candidate continuity from previous MOD releases.
4. Commercial-route monitoring.
5. CSM and Oracle Fusion as named validation targets.

### Not supported

1. Provider Fit conclusions.
2. Provider-specific flagship selection.
3. Pursue, Redirect, Reject or funded opportunity decisions.
4. Sponsor outreach.
5. Procurement or investment approval.
6. Wallet-share inference.
7. Claims requiring internal operational or classified evidence.
8. Enterprise-wide Burning Platform claims.
9. Procurement access inferred from public need or public route alone.
10. External or provider-specific use without owner review trigger.

## 10. Architecture Learning Observations

1. EU-001 is useful as a decision-boundary review lens, but it must remain subordinate to accepted architecture until the second-enterprise validation is complete.
2. The strongest benefit is not a richer narrative; it is safer separation between understanding, evidence, unknowns, contradictions and decision readiness.
3. MOD shows why enterprise need, commercial accessibility and provider fit must remain separate axes.
4. Acronym-heavy areas such as CSM require explicit disambiguation before model promotion.
5. Named technology areas such as Oracle Fusion require estate, pain, owner and route evidence before opportunity conversion.
6. Progressive assurance is preferable to full release regeneration for routine MOD refreshes.
7. The runtime should make the Decision Envelope visible beside any executive narrative.
