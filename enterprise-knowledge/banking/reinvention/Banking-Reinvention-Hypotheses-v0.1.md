## 1. Method summary

The Enterprise Growth Method states that reinvention work starts from understanding, pressure and enterprise value rather than technology alone. It defines ten reinvention domains — Customer, Product, Distribution, Operations, Decisions, Workforce, Platform, Revenue, Cost and Economics — and distinguishes the Reinvent horizon as imagining tomorrow's industry without treating desired future state as forecast.

This asset therefore treats each hypothesis as a disciplined question about how current mechanisms could evolve under observed pressure. It does not assert that the change will happen.

## BRH-001 — Primary relationship control may move from account ownership to continuous engagement

### Hypothesis Statement

Over the next 5–10 years, UK banking primary-relationship control may depend less on nominal current-account ownership alone and more on continuous digital engagement, money-flow visibility, trust and product-adjacent usage.

### Reasoning

Governed observations show that current-account primacy remains an industry control point, but high digital engagement does not itself prove primacy. Challenger evidence shows active-user primacy and engagement growth, while incumbent evidence preserves deposit/current-account economics. The hypothesis is therefore not that current accounts disappear, but that primacy may need to be evidenced through money-flow depth, engagement, product attachment and trust rather than account existence alone.

### Supporting Evidence

Industry Twin relationship-money mechanism family; Observation Register entries on primacy, relationship-money flows, digital engagement and challenger growth; Matrix correction that BM-03 is supported but not established and that digital engagement must not be equated with primacy.

### Contradicting Evidence

BK-OBS-013 states that high digital engagement does not by itself prove current-account primacy. BK-OBS-005 still preserves current-account primacy as a control point. Incumbent deposit and hedge evidence remains strong.

### Unknowns

### Confidence

**Medium** — The direction is supported by multiple observations, but the causal edge from engagement to durable primacy remains unresolved.

### Evidence Required

- Cohort-level active-user primary banking evidence
- Deposit balance depth by engagement cohort
- Longitudinal switching and multi-banking data

### Monitoring Indicators

- Published main-bank usage metrics
- Deposit growth by customer cohort
- Product attachment and direct-debit disclosures
- Regulator or market data on switching and multi-banking

### Potential Industry Impact

Would change how Banking Twins model customer value creation, from static account holdings toward relationship depth, usage, money-flow control and trust evidence.


## BRH-002 — Deposit franchise advantage may become more actively priced and less passive

### Hypothesis Statement

Deposit acquisition and pricing may become a more dynamic strategic capability in UK banking as rate sensitivity, challenger deposit growth and structural-hedge economics expose the limits of passive deposit-franchise advantage.

### Reasoning

The Observation Register preserves deposit-taking banks as the economic centre and structural hedge/NII as strong for deposit-scale models. It also records strengthening deposit pricing pressure and unresolved customer-segment duration and elasticity. The Mechanism Catalogue separates BM-06 from BM-07, meaning acquisition/pricing and hedge/investment-capacity mechanisms must be tested separately.

## BRH-013 — Mutual value allocation may remain a distinct reinvention path

### Hypothesis Statement

Mutual banking may preserve a distinct reinvention path in which surplus allocation, member value, branch trust, capital strength and transformation investment are balanced differently from shareholder-bank models.
