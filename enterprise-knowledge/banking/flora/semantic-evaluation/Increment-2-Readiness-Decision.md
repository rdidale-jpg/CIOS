## Conditions for Increment 2

1. Keep Increment 2 bounded to Evidence-Governed Explain; do not add recommendations, opportunity scoring or write-back.
2. Require every explanation to separate passage fact, derived Evidence, Observation, inference and Unknown.
3. Add a prompt guard that sector or regulatory passages cannot be converted into Lloyds-specific findings without an explicit governed relationship.
4. Add a prompt guard that risk disclosures are not evidence of realised failures unless source content says so.
5. Preserve explicit evidence demands for primary-account primacy, capital allocation, AI/cloud outcome value, workload criticality and control burden.
6. Treat the Lloyds fixture as adequate for the semantic gate but inadequate for production-grade Lloyds strategic coverage.

## Corpus adequacy

The corpus is adequate for a narrow Lloyds semantic gate because it includes company reporting, company announcement, supplier/AI activity, regulatory context, apparent contradictions, unknowns and content-specific materiality tests. It is not adequate for production-grade Lloyds explanation beyond the bounded increment.

## Semantic runtime adequacy

The semantic runtime is adequate for a bounded Evidence-Governed Explain increment when governed Mode C inputs are used and the conditions above are enforced. It is not adequate for broad enterprise understanding or recommendation generation from this fixture.

## Precise remediation required

Before or during Increment 2 implementation, add automated checks or prompt assertions for: no direct Lloyds attribution from sector-general passages without relationship lineage; no risk-factor-to-incident conversion; mandatory Unknown output; mandatory fact/Observation/inference separation; and mandatory evidence-demand output for unsupported causality.
