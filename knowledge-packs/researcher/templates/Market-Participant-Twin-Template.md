# Market Participant Twin Template

**Document ID:** TEMPLATE-Market-Participant-Twin  
**Authority:** template  
**Status:** Pack output template  
**Version:** 1.0

Do not record an absolute participant-strength conclusion. Strength must name the buyer, account need, capability or opportunity context, and must be account-relative, capability-relative and time-relative.

## 1. Participant identity

Legal identity; trading names; ownership; geography; sectors; source lineage.

## 2. Market role

Supplier, competitor, incumbent, partner, adviser, systems integrator, technology vendor, specialist challenger or other evidenced role.

## 3. Capability portfolio

Capabilities, technologies, delivery models, accreditations, constraints and confidence.

## 4. Supported buyer outcomes

Outcomes claimed or evidenced; classify as fact, participant-supplied assertion, inference, Unknown or Contradiction.

## 5. Delivery evidence

Named deliveries, programmes, customers, case evidence and independent evidence.

## 6. Buyer/account presence

Accounts, stakeholders, routes, current presence and historical presence.

## 7. Contracts and programmes

Contracts, programmes, values, dates, expiry/renewal signals and lineage.

## 8. Frameworks and procurement eligibility

Frameworks, lots, eligibility constraints, buying routes and partner routes.

## 9. Partners

Partners, consortium roles, subcontracting relationships and evidence.

## 10. Competitors

Competitors, capability overlap, differentiators and displacement risks.

## 11. Account-relative strengths

Named buyer/account need/capability/opportunity; supporting evidence; freshness; confidence.

## 12. Account-relative weaknesses

Named buyer/account need/capability/opportunity; contradictory evidence; vulnerabilities; confidence.

## 13. Access

Account access, framework access, relationship access and Unknowns.

## 14. Incumbency

Incumbent positions, renewal risk, displacement risk and timing.

## 15. Constraints

Eligibility, capacity, geographic, policy, security, commercial or delivery constraints.

## 16. Unknowns

Open questions and next learning actions.

## 17. Contradictions

Contradictory evidence and required human review.

## 18. Confidence and freshness

Confidence, freshness, observed dates and stale evidence flags.

## 19. Evidence and Observation lineage

Source IDs, Evidence IDs, Observation IDs and relationship IDs.

## 20. Human-validation state

Not reviewed, reviewed with conditions, validated, rejected or escalation required.

## 21. Structural-significance depth checklist

For structurally significant participants, confirm coverage of strategy; financial and growth pressure; capabilities and offerings; industry and account footprint; delivery evidence; incumbent positions; alliances and subcontracting; procurement routes; strengths and vulnerabilities; delivery risks; likely future moves; Unknowns and Contradictions. Record a maturity score using 0 = not researched, 1 = superficial, 2 = evidence-backed description, 3 = integrated causal and operational understanding, 4 = decision-grade temporal and executive understanding.
