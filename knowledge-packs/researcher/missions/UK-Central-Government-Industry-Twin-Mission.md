# UK Central Government Industry Twin Mission

**Document ID:** MISSION-UKCG-001  
**Status:** Mission instructions only  
**Owner:** CIOS Research Operations  
**Version:** 2.3.0

Execute the canonical Researcher route for a manageable first UK Central Government enterprise portfolio. This file contains mission instructions only and no UK Central Government research findings.

## Mission objective

Develop a UK Government Industry Twin that builds connected commercial intelligence capable of supporting relevant opportunity discovery. The objective is not merely to describe government organisations.

## Required coverage

Research industry, enterprise, participant, contract, procurement and programme context. The mission explicitly requires:

- UK Government Industry Twin development;
- prioritised buyer Enterprise Twins;
- relevant Market Participant Twins;
- major incumbent suppliers;
- challengers and potential partners;
- programmes;
- contracts;
- contract renewal and expiry signals;
- procurement frameworks and buying routes;
- buyer-supplier relationships;
- programme-capability relationships;
- supplier-framework relationships;
- account-relative participant positioning;
- evidence-backed opportunity hypotheses;
- focal-supplier relevance where a focal supplier profile is supplied.

## Bounded completion mode

The UK Central Government mission may enter bounded completion mode when a substantial working Twin exists but remains Not Research-ready because residual evidence gaps, human-validation items, contract coverage limits, framework-lot normalisation issues, trust/identity mapping gaps or BPO evidence gaps remain. In this mode the Researcher should complete the most commercially useful evidence-governed candidate Twin achievable from available public evidence, explicitly recording material residual gaps rather than iterating indefinitely.

Bounded completion preserves all evidence controls. It removes unnecessary repeated process intervention only. The Researcher may continue research autonomously, close evidence gaps in priority order, prioritise commercially material gaps over exhaustive completeness, and produce a bounded maturity assessment where Unknowns remain.

Completion does not mean that every Unknown is closed. Completion means:

- critical evidence families have been assessed against the source hierarchy and evidence threshold;
- major departments, capability clusters, procurement routes and participant relationships are represented;
- material gaps, Unknowns and Contradictions are explicitly recorded and prioritised;
- the Twin is suitable for formal maturity assessment;
- an executive-consumable output can be produced without unsupported certainty.

## Outputs

Produce Evidence records, atomic Observations, Enterprise Model updates, Market Participant Twin material, Knowledge Graph relationships, Behaviour and market-position assessment, Opportunity hypotheses, Account-Participant Position Assessments where fit is claimed, Coverage Matrix, Research Backlog, Industry Twin Maturity Assessment, Executive Intelligence Handover and Architecture Handover Report.

The maturity assessment is mandatory before Executive Intelligence handover. Executive Intelligence must answer what is changing, why it matters, where demand is concentrating, which departments and capability areas matter most, which commercial mechanisms are shaping the market, highest-confidence opportunity themes, material Unknowns and Contradictions, and proportionate executive next actions.

## Boundaries

Require human review. The Researcher produces an evidence-bounded candidate intelligence output; it does not assert Flora runtime validation, persistence, canonical acceptance, Architecture-ready status, Implementation-ready status, Pilot-ready status or supplier recommendation. Human validation state, confidence, Unknowns, Contradictions, candidate-versus-governed distinction, participant neutrality and non-claim rules must remain visible.
