# UK Central Government Industry Twin Mission

**Document ID:** MISSION-UKCG-001  
**Status:** Mission instructions only  
**Owner:** CIOS Research Operations  
**Version:** 2.3.0

Execute the canonical Researcher route for UK Central Government using the Progressive Twin Development Method in RKI-001 and RG-001. This file contains mission instructions only and no UK Central Government research findings.

## Mission objective

Develop a UK Central Government Industry Twin as a cumulative, evidence-backed, participant-neutral candidate Industry Twin. The current UKCG material must be treated as an existing research baseline, not a completed Industry Twin. The next mission must reuse prior evidence, Observations and candidate Twin state; produce a delta plan; deepen the Twin through Enterprise Twins, Market Participant Twins, control-body modelling and cross-Twin synthesis; and continue autonomously until the objective completion gates pass or a genuine external blocker is reached.

## Authority boundaries

This mission does not create a new Twin type and does not redefine Enterprise Intelligence doctrine. Preserve EI-001 for Enterprise Model and Twin state, EI-002 for graph relationships, EI-003 for behaviour, EI-012 for Evidence and Observation lineage, IT-001 and the existing Industry Twin Lifecycle guidance for Industry Twin semantics, and MPT-001 for Market Participant Twin semantics. Regulatory, assurance, policy, funding and control bodies are modelled using existing Enterprise, Market Participant, Industry Twin and relationship semantics as applicable.

## Required operating sequence

The Researcher must:

1. Load prior UKCG governed and candidate state, prior Evidence records, prior Observations, prior source maps, prior Unknowns, prior Contradictions and any previous candidate handover.
2. Produce an incremental delta plan that identifies reused baseline material, stale material, evidence needing reconfirmation, missing Enterprise depth, missing participant/control-body depth and missing relationships.
3. Create an initial government-wide industry and three-horizon frame across Operate, Transform and Reinvent.
4. Determine Tier 1 and Tier 2 organisations from evidence using structural significance: mandate, spend, citizen or service impact, policy control, programme centrality, supplier concentration, data or identity dependency, assurance power and contradiction risk.
5. Build deep Enterprise Twins for all Tier 1 organisations before treating the Industry Twin as complete.
6. Build material supplier, Market Participant, regulatory, assurance, policy, funding and control-body Twin material where structurally significant.
7. Create a connected government transformation programme portfolio across three horizons.
8. Model programme, funding, policy, supplier, capability, procurement, data, identity, assurance and dependency relationships.
9. Derive industry relevance only after participant-neutral cross-Twin synthesis.
10. Reassess breadth, depth, Unknowns, Contradictions and executive insight quality after each research wave.
11. Select the next highest-impact incomplete area and continue without requiring a separate user conversation for each organisation, supplier, programme or evidence gap.
12. Stop only when the completion gates pass or a genuine external blocker is documented.

Research industry, enterprise, participant, contract, procurement, programme, funding, policy, assurance, regulatory and control-body context. The mission explicitly requires:

- UK Central Government Industry Twin development;

- three-horizon Operate / Transform / Reinvent map;
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 organisation register derived from evidence;
- deep Tier 1 Enterprise Twins;
- material Market Participant Twins;
- major incumbent suppliers;

- regulatory, assurance, policy, funding and control bodies;
- transformation programmes;

## Tier 1 Enterprise Twin completion standard

Each Tier 1 Enterprise Twin must cover: purpose, mandate and policy outcomes; citizens, customers, users and beneficiaries; financial position, expenditure and funding pressure; operating and service-delivery model; leadership, organisation and governance; workforce, skills and productivity; technology, infrastructure and legacy estate; data, identity, trust and information governance; supplier ecosystem, contracts and procurement; performance, risk, resilience and behaviour; transformation programmes across Operate, Transform and Reinvent; dependencies, relationships, Unknowns and Contradictions.

Apply the maturity rubric: 0 not researched; 1 superficial; 2 evidence-backed description; 3 integrated causal and operational understanding; 4 decision-grade temporal and executive understanding. Tier 1 completion requires no domain below 2; finance, operating model and transformation portfolio at least 3; overall maturity at least 3; explicit evidence lineage; explicit Unknowns and Contradictions; and a coherent three-horizon trajectory.

## Transformation programme standard

Every material programme must connect business or policy goal → pressure or failure mechanism → programme → accountable enterprise and leadership → funding context → operating-model change → technology and data dependencies → delivery participants → procurement route → dependencies → risks and contradictions → current state → Operate / Transform / Reinvent horizon → expected enterprise outcome.

## Market Participant and control-body depth standard

Structurally significant participant Twins must cover strategy; financial and growth pressure; capabilities and offerings; industry and account footprint; delivery evidence; incumbent positions; alliances and subcontracting; procurement routes; strengths and vulnerabilities; delivery risks; likely future moves; Unknowns and Contradictions.

Control-body material must cover mandate, decision rights, policy or assurance mechanism, affected enterprises and programmes, funding or approval influence, data or reporting dependencies, relationship evidence, behavioural incentives, risks, Unknowns and Contradictions. Use existing canonical semantics; do not introduce a separate Twin type.

## Autonomous continuation and objective completion gates

The Researcher must maintain and repeatedly update: scope and tiering register; enterprise maturity matrix; participant maturity matrix; transformation programme portfolio; three-horizon map; coverage matrix; Unknown and Contradiction register; evidence demand queue; cross-Twin synthesis; executive insight quality assessment; completion-gate report; and incremental change log from the prior baseline.

The mission must not stop merely because a long report has been written, a record target has been met, every named entity has one observation, the first source list is exhausted, or a package can be produced. The Researcher must automatically choose the next highest-impact incomplete area and continue.

Completion gates pass only when:

- the prior UKCG baseline has been explicitly reused or dispositioned in the delta plan;
- the government-wide industry frame and three-horizon map are present;
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 organisations are derived from evidence and tiering rationale is recorded;
- all Tier 1 Enterprise Twins meet the Tier 1 completion standard;
- structurally significant Market Participant and control-body material meets the required depth standard or has a blocker record;
- all material programmes meet the transformation programme standard or have a blocker record;
- programme, funding, policy, supplier, capability, procurement and dependency relationships are modelled with explicit lineage;
- Unknowns and Contradictions are visible, prioritised and tied to maturity consequences;
- executive insights pass the executive insight gate;
- industry relevance is derived from participant-neutral cross-Twin synthesis;
- completion-gate decision states pass, bounded release with conditions, or blocked.

A genuine blocker must document missing evidence, materiality, sources attempted, maturity consequence and whether a bounded release remains possible.

## Consolidated handover output

Produce one consolidated handover containing: updated Industry Twin; detailed Enterprise Twin set; detailed Market Participant and control-body Twin set; relational and dependency model; government transformation portfolio across Operate, Transform and Reinvent; maturity and coverage assessment; executive insight set; industry-relevance assessment; Unknowns and Contradictions; evidence demands; completion-gate decision; and incremental change log from the prior baseline.

Packaging remains secondary to intelligence quality. The maturity assessment is mandatory before Executive Intelligence handover.

## Boundaries

Require human review. The Researcher produces an evidence-bounded candidate intelligence output; it does not assert Flora runtime validation, persistence, canonical acceptance, Architecture-ready status, Implementation-ready status, Pilot-ready status or supplier recommendation. Human validation state, confidence, Unknowns, Contradictions, candidate-versus-governed distinction, participant neutrality and non-claim rules must remain visible. Do not rebuild the UKCG Twin inside this mission instruction; use the mission to guide the next research execution.
